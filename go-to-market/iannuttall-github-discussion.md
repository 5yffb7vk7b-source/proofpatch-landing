**Suggested title:** Do you actually want a record of what your agent did, or is that overkill?

**Body:**

Wanted a sanity check from people who actually live in this workflow rather than me guessing.

When a coding agent finishes a task you get the git diff — what changed. What you don't really
get is what *happened* around it: which commands it ran, what it tried and backed out of, what
got blocked along the way. I keep flip-flopping on whether that after-the-run evidence is
genuinely useful or just overkill that nobody asks for.

So, honest question: when you let an agent run, do you ever wish you had a record of what it
actually did, or is the diff plus "tests pass" enough in practice? Not selling anything here —
just trying to figure out if this is a real itch or one I've talked myself into.
